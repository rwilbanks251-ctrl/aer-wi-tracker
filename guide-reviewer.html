Work Instruction Reviewer Guide

For Andrew & Paola — how to find what's waiting on your review and how to close it out.

1

Open the Google Sheet and request access

Work Instructions Google Sheet
If you don't have access yet, click Request Access — Rob will approve it.

2

Filter to see what's waiting on you

In the sheet, filter the Status column to Andrew Paola Review — that's your queue. (The Assigned To column tracks the team building each WI, not the reviewer, so filter by status here rather than by name.)

3

Open the Document Link

Use the Document Link column on each row — that's the one shared template being reviewed, so any comments or notes land in the same place everyone else is working from.

4

Review it, then notify Rob either way

Read through the Work Instruction against the standard below, then tell Rob what you found — he moves it to the next stage from there.

Acceptable — notify Rob it's approved. He moves it forward to QCBD Approval.
Needs revisions — notify Rob what needs fixing. He kicks it back to the assignee (P2 Rework) with your notes.

The standard to review against

Every Work Instruction must be written so that someone with zero prior knowledge could walk in off the street and perform the job using only the document.

Check for missing steps — not just the obvious ones, but the ones that feel automatic to someone experienced: every click, every field, every physical action, every judgment call. If a step is assumed rather than written down, that's a revision.
