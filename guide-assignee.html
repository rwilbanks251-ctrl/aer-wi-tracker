Work Instruction Assignee Guide

How to find your tasks, work from the right source, and turn in a finished Work Instruction.

1

Open the Google Sheet and request access

Work Instructions – Detailed Tracker (Google Sheet)
If you don't have access yet, click Request Access on the page — Rob will approve it.

2

Filter to your name

Use the Assigned To filter (in the sheet, or on the live tracker) to see just your open Work Instructions and where each one currently stands.

3

Work from the live Document Link

Each row has a Document Link column — that's the one shared file for your WI. Work in it directly rather than a personal copy, so everyone stays organized and nothing gets duplicated or lost.

4

Fill in the template like the example

Use the blank EHS-F100-4 template and the worked example pinned in the Teams chat as your model — same structure, same level of detail, same step/photo format.

5

Notify Rob when it's complete

Once your Work Instruction is finished, let Rob know directly — he moves it into review from there.

The standard every WI must meet

Write it so that someone with zero prior knowledge could walk in off the street and perform the job using only your Work Instruction.

Do not leave out any steps — not the ones that feel obvious to you, not the ones you do automatically after months on the job. If a step exists, it goes in the document: every click, every field, every physical action, every judgment call you'd normally make on instinct.
